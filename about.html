About Me

I'm a recent Carleton College grad with a love for algorithms, mathematical reasoning, and problem solving. Equipped with a knack for analytical logic from my theoretical math days, I now dive deep into biological sciences such as genetics and neuroscience.

As a native Minnesotan, the great outdoors is where I'm happiest. Whether I'm running, hiking, or simply enjoying a good book, you'll likely find me soaking up the fresh air. And if you spot me in the summer, don't hesitate to ask about my super stylish watch tan!

My main hobbies are reading, running, and puzzle solving. Check out what I read under the "Projects" tab! When it comes to puzzle solving, crosswords are my bread and butter, but I also love a variant sudoku. I've gotten quick, solving the New York Times Monday crossword in 2 minutes, 45 seconds. For variant sudokus, I play puzzles from Cracking the Cryptic. I've got some tools to help me in solving under "Projects" as well.

When interacting with humans, I'm fluent in Russian and proficient in French. If I'm communicating with a computer, I can do it in Python, Java, C++, LaTeX, and Excel. I'm also capable in HTML, CSS, and Javascript, as evidenced by this very website.
