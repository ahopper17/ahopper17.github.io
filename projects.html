In the mean time, check out this video of my Natural Selection simulator at work!

Each green square is a food source that is randomly chosen by the simulator. An organism eats when it lands on a food source, using up the resources in that cell and adding that to their overall energy. Food sources replenish. If an organism's energy is high enough, it reproduces (there is also a chance of reproducing if the organism's energy is relatively high but not yet at reproduction threshold). At each reproduction, there is a 5% chance of mutating to a faster speed. An organism with faster speed can move more cells per frame, consuming any food source it lands on. Pretty neat!
